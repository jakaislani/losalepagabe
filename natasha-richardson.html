Natasha Jane Richardson was an English actress who worked on stage and on the screen. She was born on 11th May 1963 and died on 18th March 2009. She was a member of the Redgrave family. She was born to the well-known actress Vanessa Redgrave and Tony Richardson (a well-known director and producer). While Michael Redgrave and Rachel Kempson were grandparents.

Career

Early in her career, she played Mary Shelley in Ken Russell’s Gothic (1986) and Patty Hearst in a 1988 biopic directed by Paul Schrader, and later she won a Theatre World Award for her Broadway debut in 1993 revival of Anna Christie.

Additionally, she appeared in The Handmaid’s Tale (1990), Nell (1994), The Parent Trap (1998), Maid in Manhattan (2002), and The White Countess (2005).

As Sally Bowles in the 1998 Broadway revival of Cabaret, she won the Tony Award for Best Performance by a Leading Actress in a Musical, the Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Actress in a Musical, and the Outer Critics Circle Award.

However, Natasha Richardson established herself as the iconic character of the Hollywood film and entertainment industry where she did numerous projects and won the hearts of many.
