How many times has Michael Buffer said Let's get ready to rumble?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, July 16, 2024

Michael Buffer is a multi-talented American ring announcer for various sports disciplines such as boxing, wrestling, etc.

He was born on November 2, 1944, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Let’s get ready to rumble catchphrase was coined by him years ago.

Table of Content

Does Michael Buffer own Let’s get ready to rumble?

The catchphrase was loved by many people around the globe when he started using it as a ring announcer. This prompted him to patent it as a trademark.

He kickstarted the process of owning the phrase in the 80s and finally succeeded in owning it in 1992 when he was granted a federal trademark license for it.

Since then his trademark catchphrase has earned him over $400 million dollars.

How much does Buffer make every time he says let’s get ready to rumble?

His fee for uttering those five words varies greatly depending on the occasion. According to our findings, his minimum charge is currently $25,000.

Some event organizers have gone ahead to pay him a whopping $100,000 just to blurt out the catchphrase in the unique way that we have become accustomed to.

How many times has Michael Buffer said ‘Let’s get ready to rumble?

It will be super tough to put a definite figure on how many times he has used his catchphrase.

He has over the years announced so many important fights it’s just almost impossible to count.

Additionally, Buffer has announced matches on-air for the Steve Albert and Jesse Ventura-hosted syndicated game show The Grudge Match.

On December 10, 2007, he opened the American Idol season finale and made an appearance on NBC’s Deal or No Deal.
