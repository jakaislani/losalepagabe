Biography

RaaShaun Casey (born September 3, 1977), the artist better known as DJ Envy, is an American DJ, a skilled record producer, and a charismatic radio personality.

DJ Envy‘s rise in the hip-hop world was swift and spectacular. He quickly became one of the most recognized DJs in the country, earning a well-deserved spot among the industry’s elite. His collaborations have spanned the industry’s biggest names, from Jay-Z to Kanye West and Lil Wayne, solidifying his position as a major player in the music scene.

What sets DJ Envy apart is his enduring impact. His radio show continues to captivate audiences with its musical offerings, maintaining his strong presence in the hearts of listeners everywhere. His catalog of chart-topping hits speaks to his incredible talent and ability to collaborate with some of the most celebrated artists in the business.

Early Life & Education

DJ Envy, born in the heart of New York City on September 3, 1977, rose as a prominent figure in the music scene. With roots linked to Janet Casey, his mother, and a former police officer father, his journey began.

DJ Envy‘s career flourished in hip-hop. He crafted mixtapes that resonated with fans and collaborated with stars such as Jay-Z, Kanye West, and Lil Wayne.

DJ Envy shares a tight bond with Charlamagne tha God, not by blood but through their roles as co-hosts on the “Breakfast Club.”

Career

DJ Envy, a musical star, has made an indelible mark on the radio scene, mainly through his role on The Breakfast Club. His journey to prominence began in mixtape production, where he featured exclusive freestyles from artists like Jay-Z, Fabolous, and 50 Cent. His prowess earned him three Justo Mixtape Awards and the prestigious Most Influential Radio D.J. Award in 2008.

Born RaaShaun Casey, DJ Envy inherited a passion for music from his family and showed a knack for crafting beats from a young age. Networking diligently, he found his place at Hot 97, where he met Angela Yee and Charlamagne tha God, future co-hosts on The Breakfast Club.

In 2003, his mixtape DJ Envy-Block Party, Vol. 1, peaked at number 57 on the “Billboard Top 200,” expanding his audience. He excelled, co-hosting morning programs and becoming a prominent voice in New York’s hip-hop scene. His influence extended to Sirius XM and Power 105.

What sets DJ Envy apart is his commitment to education. He earned a degree in audio engineering from the Institute of Audio Research in New York City, elevating his skills.

Beyond music, DJ Envy is a philanthropist, organizing charity events for underprivileged children and championing mental health awareness. In 2020, he was inducted into the Radio Hall of Fame.

DJ Envy‘s most recent venture is co-authoring the book “Real Life, Real Love: Life Lessons on Joy, Pain, and the Magic That Holds Us Together” with his wife, Gia Casey.

Awards & Nominations

DJ Envy‘s hard work and dedication have not gone unnoticed; he has received several awards and nominations. In 2014, he won the BET Award for Best DJ of the Year, and in 2015, he was nominated for the iHeartRadio Music Award for Best Hip-Hop/R&B DJ. 

DJ Envy has also been recognized for his philanthropic efforts, receiving the Key to the City of Brooklyn in 2016 for his work in the community.

DJ Envy‘s now happily married to Gia Casey; they have two daughters: Brooklynn Jagger Casey and London Casey.

DJ Envy frequently shares glimpses of his personal life on social media, highlighting his strong bond with his family. Despite his busy schedule, he values quality time with his loved ones.

Moreover, he’s actively involved in philanthropy and serves on the board of directors for the Children’s Aid Society. DJ Envy‘s commitment to giving back is evident through his hosting of charity events for organizations like the Make-A-Wish Foundation and the American Cancer Society.
