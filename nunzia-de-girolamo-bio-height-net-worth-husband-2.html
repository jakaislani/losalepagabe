Career

Nunzia De Girolamo joined the Forza Italia party and entered politics in 2007. She did, however, leave the Forza Italia Party in 2009. She then won a seat in the Italian parliament as a representative of the People of Freedom party.

Nunzia De Girolamo also participated in Silvio Berlusconi’s People of Freedom (PdL) movement at the time. She then won elections for the parliament in 2008 and 2013 and was seated in that position.

After four years, in October of that same year, she left The People of Freedom party. She previously refuted reports of leaving the PdL in October 2013. 2013 saw her join the “New Centre-Right,” a new Italian political party founded by Angelino Alfano. She was appointed to the cabinet on April 28, 2013, to serve as the Minister of Agricultural, Food, and Forestry Policies.

Enrico Letta also led the ministries after Mario Catania left the position. She was also the youngest and one of seven female members of the Letta government. On January 26, 2014, she did quit her position in response to accusations of inappropriate behavior on her part. She also submitted her resignation on January 27, 2014, and Enrico Letta, the acting prime minister, accepted it.

Nunzia De Girolamo was also the second cabinet minister to step down after the elections in April 2013. She was then chosen to serve as the New Centre-Right Party’s House Whip. She left the New Centre-Right Party in 2015 and returned to Forza Italia, the organization where she first started her political career.
